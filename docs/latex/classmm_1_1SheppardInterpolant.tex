class mm\+::\+Sheppard\+Interpolant$<$ vec\+\_\+t, value\+\_\+t $>$}
Scattered interpolant using a slightly modified Sheppard\textquotesingle{}s interpolation (inverse distance weighting). 

The essential difference is the introduction of a "{}regularization"{} parameter ({\ttfamily reg}) that is used when computing the inverse distance weights\+: $ w_i = 1 / (d(x, x_i)^{power} + reg) $ By supplying a positive regularization parameter one can avoid singularities at the locations of the data points as well as control the "{}smoothness"{} of the interpolation (e.\+g., make the weights of the neighbors less varied). The "{}smoothness"{} of interpolation can also be controlled by the power parameter ({\ttfamily power}). The interpolation is not continuous due to the cut off at the closest neighbors.
